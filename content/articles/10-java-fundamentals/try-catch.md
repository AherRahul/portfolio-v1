When dealing with the realm of programming, exceptions are an inevitable part of the journey. They can crop up when you least expect them, potentially derailing your application if not handled correctly.

That’s where the **try-catch block** comes into play, acting like a safety net that allows your code to handle these unexpected scenarios gracefully.

This chapter will delve into the ins and outs of try-catch blocks in Java, showing you how to effectively manage exceptions and keep your applications running smoothly, even when things go awry.
